Sacramento, the capital of California!

Welcome to Sacramento, the energetic capital of California, incorporated on February 27, 1850. Currently, Sacramento has a population of 518,605. You can find Sacramento in the Sacramento Valley region of California. Sacramento is an urban city, but also has a large suburban population. The average household income level in Sacramento is $93,320, compared to California's average of $119,149 for household income.

About Sacramento

Sacramento is where attractiveness of the present era coexists with history! Sacramento, also known as "The City of Trees," is a sunny city with a diverse range of activities to suit the needs of any kind of tourist. Discover artifacts from the Gold Rush era or visit the Transcontinental Railroad's and witness amazing engineering. Sacramento has a thriving cultural environment that includes theaters, community events, and well-known museums like the Crocker Art Museum. In addition to its cultural attractions, Sacramento offers breathtaking landscapes and expansive parks. The beautiful Sacramento River which provides chances for kayaking, leisurely cruises, and beautiful walks along its banks.

Fun Facts

  1. Solar-Powered Stadium: Sacramento boasts the Golden 1 Center, the first ever National Basketball Association (NBA) arena to be entirely powered by solar energy (Upal, 2017).
  2. World's Oldest Triathlon (Without Swimming): Sacramento is home to "Eppie's Great Race," the world's oldest continuously running triathlon. This unique race features running, cycling, and kayaking instead of swimming (Flynn, 2021).
  3. Global Peace Symbol: Sacramento is home to three of the nine International World Peace Rose Gardens. These gardens serve as a symbol of peace and hope, and are a beautiful sight to behold. (Jinan-sacramento sister city world peace rose garden - 2018)

Fun Locations

  1. California State Railroad Museum: Explore rich railroad history, featuring restored locomotives, interactive exhibits, and scenic train rides.
  2. State Capitol: Guided tours are available to learn more about the history and significance of the capitol building.
  3. Crocker Museum of Art: The museum houses a vast collection of over 150,000 works of art, spanning centuries and cultures.
  4. Fairytale Town: Explore miniature storybook settings, ride a carousel, and climb a giant sculpture of Swiss cheese!
  5. Sacramento Zoo: Explore various habitats, watch animal feedings, and learn about conservation efforts.
