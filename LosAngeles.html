Los Angeles

Los Angeles, known as the entertainment capital of the world was incorporated on April 4, 1850. Currently, Los Angeles has a population of 3,902,440. You can find Los Angeles in the Southern California region. Los Angeles consists of a primarily urban population. The average household income level in Los Angeles is $106,931, close to California's average of $119,149 for household income.

About Los Angeles

Sunshine-drenched Los Angeles provides a multitude of experiences. Explore the famous Hollywood Walk of Fame, impressive neighborhoods, and famous sites. There will be leading museums, live acts, and breathtaking scenery. Enjoy a leisurely stroll or bike along well-known beaches such as Venice Beach Every interest is catered to in L.A. Savor fresh seafood at the Santa Monica Pier, experience the exciting nightlife and BBQ of Koreatown, or treat yourself to fine dining in Beverly Hills.

Fun Facts

  1. The now famous Hollywood sign was originally just an advertisement supposed to promote a new housing development in 1923 and it used to be "HollywoodLand" (Oaks, 2023).
  2. Although Hollywood is now regarded as the American film capital, Atlantic City, New Jersey was the country's film capital before Los Angeles (Oaks, 2023).
  3. An internationally recognized attraction, the Santa Monica Pier welcomes millions of tourists each year. The ferris wheel at Pacific Park with its twelve rides, midway games, specialty restaurants by the ocean, and waterfront shopping, may be the most famous attraction of them all. It's a little-known fact that this wheel is the first and only solar-powered Ferris wheel in the world. (Christina, 2022)

Fun Locations

  1. Hollywood Walk of Fame: Stroll down the iconic Hollywood Walk of Fame, searching for the stars of your favorite celebrities embedded in the sidewalk.
  2. Dolby Theatre: Take a behind-the-scenes tour or attend a live performance at the Dolby Theatre, home to the Oscars.
  3. Warner Bros. Studio Tour Hollywood:Go on a guided studio tour at Warner Bros. Studio Hollywood, where legendary films and TV shows are made. Get a peek behind the scenes and see sets, props, and costumes from your favorite productions.
  4. Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA):Delve into a vast collection of art at LACMA, featuring works from around the world and across various periods. The museum's Urban and Resnick Pavilion, designed by architect Rem Koolhaas, is a landmark itself.
  5. Santa Monica Beach:Spend a day at the beach in Santa Monica, swimming, surfing, sunbathing, or simply strolling along the pier with its amusement park and Pacific Park Ferris wheel.
